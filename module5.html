üìã Subject Access Request Procedure

STEP 1: Verify Identity

Always verify the person's identity first before sending any personal data. Ask for ID or security questions.

STEP 2: Search All Systems

Look in: customer database, order history, complaint records, marketing lists, any notes about allergies/preferences.

STEP 3: Compile Data

Gather ALL personal information we hold about them - even handwritten notes count as personal data.

STEP 4: Remove Third Party Data

If our records mention other people, redact those names before sending.

DEADLINE: 1 Month

You have 1 month from receiving the request to provide the data. This is free for the customer.

üö´ Marketing Objection Procedure

STEP 1: Stop Marketing Immediately

When someone objects to marketing, you must stop sending promotional emails right away - not next week or next month.

STEP 2: Check What They Want

Ask: "Do you want us to stop ALL emails, or just marketing? We may still need to contact you about orders you place."

STEP 3: Legitimate Business Needs

You CAN keep their email for: order confirmations, delivery updates, safety recalls, account security. You CANNOT use it for: newsletters, promotions, "you might like" emails.

STEP 4: Update Preferences

Mark them as "marketing objection" in your system. Train staff to never add them back to marketing lists.

DEADLINE: Immediate

Marketing objections must be acted on immediately - not within a month.

üë• Employee Data Request Procedure

RECTIFICATION (Correcting Data)

Emergency contacts: Verify it's really the employee asking, then update immediately - it's a safety issue. Bank details: Verify identity + get written confirmation. Personal details: Check ID then update.

ACCESS (Getting Copy of Data)

Employees have the right to see their own: employment contract, performance reviews, training records, disciplinary records, absence records, payroll information.

PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

YES, employees can get copies of their own performance reviews. It's their personal data. You don't need manager permission - the employee has a legal right to see it.

WHAT TO REDACT

Remove other employees' names/comments, but keep the employee's own information visible. They have a right to see what's written about them.

URGENT vs ROUTINE

Safety issues (emergency contacts): Update immediately. Everything else: 1 month deadline for providing copies.

‚ö†Ô∏è Employees have the same data rights as customers - don't treat them differently!

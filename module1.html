PHASE 1: KNOW YOUR ENEMY

Understanding Email-Based Threats

üìß

PHISHING ATTACKS

HIGH RISK

Method: Fraudsters impersonate trusted sources to steal credentials or sensitive data.

Target: Employee login details, customer information, financial data.

Warning Signs:

  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Urgent language and time pressure
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Requests for personal information
üéØ

SPEAR PHISHING

CRITICAL

Method: Highly targeted attacks using personal information about you or Hilltop Honey.

Target: Specific employees, often management or finance teams.

Warning Signs:

  • Uses your name and job title
  • References company projects or colleagues
  • Appears to come from senior management
  • Requests urgent financial transactions
ü¶†

MALWARE DELIVERY

HIGH RISK

Method: Malicious software delivered through email attachments or links.

Target: Computer systems, production networks, customer databases.

Warning Signs:

  • Unexpected attachments (.exe, .zip, .scr files)
  • Links to unfamiliar websites
  • Emails from unknown senders
  • Files requiring immediate download
üíº

BUSINESS EMAIL COMPROMISE

CRITICAL

Method: Attackers compromise legitimate business email accounts to conduct fraud.

Target: Financial transactions, supplier payments, customer orders.

Warning Signs:

  • Unusual payment requests from suppliers
  • Changes to bank details or payment methods
  • Urgent requests from "management"
  • Emails sent outside normal business hours

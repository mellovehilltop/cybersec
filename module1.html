PHASE 1: KNOW YOUR ENEMY

Understanding Email-Based Threats

📧

PHISHING ATTACKS

HIGH RISK

Method: Fraudsters impersonate trusted sources to steal credentials or sensitive data.

Target: Employee login details, customer information, financial data.

Warning Signs:

  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
  • Urgent language requiring immediate action
  • Suspicious links or attachments
  • Poor spelling and grammar
  • Requests for sensitive information
🎯

SPEAR PHISHING

CRITICAL

Method: Highly targeted attacks using personal information to seem legitimate.

Target: Specific individuals, often senior staff or those with access to sensitive systems.

Warning Signs:

  • Uses your name and job title correctly
  • References recent company events or projects
  • Appears to come from colleagues or superiors
  • Unusual requests from familiar contacts
  • Pressure to act quickly on "confidential" matters
🦠

MALWARE ATTACHMENTS

HIGH RISK

Method: Malicious files disguised as legitimate documents or applications.

Target: System access, data theft, ransomware deployment.

Warning Signs:

  • Unexpected attachments from unknown senders
  • Files with double extensions (.pdf.exe)
  • Executables (.exe, .scr, .bat) from external sources
  • Password-protected archives without prior notice
  • Documents requesting to "enable macros"
💼

BUSINESS EMAIL COMPROMISE

CRITICAL

Method: Criminals impersonate executives to authorize fraudulent payments.

Target: Financial transactions, invoice fraud, wire transfers.

Warning Signs:

  • Unusual payment requests from suppliers
  • Changes to bank details or payment methods
  • Urgent requests from "management"
  • Emails sent outside normal business hours
  • Requests to bypass normal approval processes

PHASE 2: SPOT THE RED FLAGS

Learning to Identify Suspicious Emails

🚩 Critical Red Flags Checklist

Every agent must memorize these warning signs. Look for combinations of red flags - the more present, the higher the threat level.

SENDER ANALYSIS

  • 🔍
    Domain Mismatch

    Email claims to be from Hilltop but comes from hiltophonney.com or similar

  • 👤
    Generic Display Names

    "Admin", "Security Team", "IT Support" without specific contact details

  • 📍
    External Senders

    Internal communications coming from external email addresses

CONTENT RED FLAGS

  • ⚡
    Urgency Pressure

    "Act now", "Within 24 hours", "Account will be suspended"

  • 💰
    Financial Requests

    Unexpected payment changes, wire transfers, gift card purchases

  • 🔐
    Credential Requests

    Asking for passwords, login details, or personal information

TECHNICAL INDICATORS

  • 🔗
    Suspicious Links

    URLs that don't match the claimed destination when hovered over

  • 📎
    Unexpected Attachments

    Files you weren't expecting, especially executables or archives

  • ✍️
    Poor Quality

    Spelling errors, formatting issues, or unprofessional presentation

PHASE 3: DEFENSIVE PROTOCOLS

Safe Email Practices for Agents

✅ BEFORE YOU CLICK

👀

VERIFY THE SENDER

Check the full email address, not just the display name. Look for subtle misspellings in domains.

🔍

HOVER OVER LINKS

Never click immediately. Hover to see the real destination. Look for URL shorteners or suspicious domains.

🤔

QUESTION URGENCY

Legitimate urgent requests rarely come via email alone. Verify through phone or in person.

🛡️

VERIFY ATTACHMENTS

Only open attachments you are expecting. Scan all files with antivirus before opening.

📞

CONFIRM BY PHONE

For financial requests or sensitive information, always verify through a separate communication channel.

🔐

PROTECT CREDENTIALS

Never provide passwords or login details via email. Legitimate IT will never ask for these.

🚨 IF YOU SUSPECT A THREAT

1

STOP

Do NOT reply, click, or download. Do NOT reply to the email.

2

ISOLATE

Mark the email as spam/phishing. Do not delete it yet - IT may need to investigate.

3

REPORT

Forward the suspicious email to IT support immediately. Include your concerns in the message.

4

WARN OTHERS

If the email appears to target your department or colleagues, alert them verbally or through secure channels.

🔒 DAILY EMAIL SECURITY HABITS

Regular Updates: Keep your email client and antivirus software updated.
Strong Passwords: Use unique, strong passwords with two-factor authentication.
Clean Inbox: Regularly clean out and organize emails to spot anomalies.
Education: Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics and threats.
